PHYSIX vs PhET
Honest Comparison

PhET is the gold standard of physics simulations. PHYSIX is a new contender with a different philosophy. Here's exactly how they compare — and which to use when.

TL;DR Use PhET for polished, guided simulations on specific topics with built-in teacher resources. Use PHYSIX when you want a freeform sandbox to build, tweak, and experiment with any physics scenario from scratch.
Overview

What Each Tool Is

PHYSIX SimVerseLab

A general-purpose physics sandbox. You drag objects and forces onto a canvas and build any scenario you can imagine. There are no guided instructions — just you, the physics engine, and the equations.

PhET Colorado Uni

A collection of 150+ individual simulations, each built for one specific topic (e.g. "Projectile Motion", "Faraday's Law"). Each sim has a curated UI designed to teach that one concept.

When to use which

The Right Tool for the Job

Choose PHYSIX when...

You want to build a custom scenario — e.g. "what if I add a second star to the solar system?" You need speed control, trails, and live energy graphs. You want all forces in one place, not a separate sim per topic. You want offline use as a single file.

Choose PhET when...

You need a polished, guided activity with a teacher worksheet. You're covering a very specific topic (circuit building, acid-base reactions) that PHYSIX doesn't include. Your students are on tablets or phones.

Verdict

They're Complementary, Not Competing

The best physics classrooms use both. Start a topic with a PhET guided activity to build foundational understanding. Then open PHYSIX and let students go off-script — change parameters, build their own scenarios, ask "what if" questions. The freeform sandbox is where deep understanding forms.
