Use Cases

Who Uses
PHYSIX

From Year 9 classrooms to university research labs, PHYSIX fits wherever physics needs to be seen and felt rather than just read.

Education

Secondary & High School Physics

PHYSIX is used in BINAS-linked Dutch secondary schools, IB schools, GCSE courses, and AP Physics curricula. Every preset is tagged to a specific curriculum concept.

Live whiteboard demos

Load a preset in 3 seconds and project it on the board. Students watch equations evolve in real time — the energy readout in the topbar shows E_kin, E_pot, and E_total continuously.

Student investigation tasks

Assign students a PHYSIX experiment: "Find the launch angle that maximises range on the Moon." They adjust parameters, record results, and compare to formula predictions.

BINAS formula verification

Every preset fires a notification with the relevant BINAS formula. Students can run the simulation and check that the numbers match their formula booklet — bridging theory and practice.

Flipped classroom

Share a link to a specific preset. Students explore it at home before the lesson and arrive with observations and questions instead of blank faces.

University

Higher Education & Research

N-body simulation studies

Use PHYSIX as a quick sanity-check tool before running full numerical simulations. The 4th-order RK integrator gives reliable short-to-medium timescale results for gravitational systems.

EM field visualisation

Demonstrate Coulomb scattering, cyclotron motion, and Lenz's law to first-year students visually, then have them derive the mathematics from what they see.

Chaos theory demonstrations

The double pendulum preset is an ideal classroom tool for demonstrating sensitive dependence on initial conditions without complex numerical tools.

Rocket propulsion courses

The rocket body models Tsiolkovsky's equation directly. Students can dial in exhaust velocity and mass flow rate and see the resulting Δv in real time.

Engineering & Industry

Rapid Prototyping

Spring system design

Prototype spring-mass-damper systems before writing any code. Tune k and damping coefficients visually until the response looks right, then transcribe the values.

Collision testing

Model restitution coefficients for packaging design. Load three spheres with different e values and drop them from the same height — see which material absorbs the most energy.

Self-learners

Curiosity-driven Exploration

Some of the most creative uses come from people who aren't studying physics formally — they're just curious.

Community favourites What happens if you drop a feather and a bowling ball in a vacuum? (Load the Feather vs Ball preset, toggle atmosphere off.)

Can a small moon orbit a black hole without being torn apart? (Load the Black Hole preset, add a planet body at different distances, adjust mass.)

How many Earths fit inside Jupiter? (Use the ruler tool in True Scale mode.)
