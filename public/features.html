Forces — 7 categories

Every Force Your Experiment Needs

⚫ Gravity (GRV)

Uniform g, Newton's universal gravitation (1/r²), and Schwarzschild GR corrections. Apply to any body independently.

💧 Fluid (FLD)

Stokes drag, turbulent drag (Cd·A), buoyancy, and viscosity. Configure air density or liquid medium.

⚙ Mechanical (MCH)

Hookean spring (k, L₀, damping), rigid rod, constant thrust, and user-scripted custom forces.

⚡ Electromagnetic (EM)

Coulomb (kq₁q₂/r²), Lorentz (q·v×B), uniform E field, magnetic braking (Lenz's law).

🔥 Thermal (THM)

Temperature-dependent drag, blackbody radiation pressure, and ablation mass loss for re-entry scenarios.

⚛ Nuclear (NUC)

Strong nuclear (Yukawa), weak decay chain, and fission fragment recoil. Useful for particle physics visualisations.

✎ Custom (FX)

Write any F(t, r, v) expression in the formula editor. Supports all standard math functions and body properties.

Measurement tools

Built-in Lab Instruments

📏

Ruler

Click-drag to measure any distance on canvas in metres, km, AU, or light-years depending on scale.

📐

Angle Tool

Three-point angle measurement. Useful for incline angles and orbit eccentricity.

◎

Camera Follow

Lock the viewport to any body. Essential for watching projectile arcs or orbital paths from the reference frame of the body.

Live Graphs

12 plot types: position, velocity, acceleration, kinetic/potential/total energy, angular momentum, temperature, charge, and more.

Energy Readout

Topbar shows running totals for E_kin, E_pot, and E_total. Conservation of energy is directly observable.

Time Display

Simulation clock shows t in seconds, minutes, hours, days, years, or Gyr — automatically scaled to scenario.
